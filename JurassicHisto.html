🦕 The Jurassic Period

201 – 145 million years ago

The Jurassic Period lasted about 56 million years, from 201 million years ago (mya) to 145 mya. It followed the Triassic-Jurassic extinction event that cleared the way for dinosaurs to become the undisputed rulers of the land.It is best remembered as the “Golden Age of Dinosaurs.”

201–174
mya

🌋 Early Jurassic

Recovery from Extinction

The Triassic-Jurassic extinction had wiped out many competitors, allowing dinosaurs to expand rapidly across Pangaea.

🌋 Early Jurassic

Continents & Climate

Pangaea began to break apart, forming the beginnings of the Atlantic Ocean.Climate was warm, humid, and more stable than the dry Triassic.Coastal environments supported lush vegetation.

🌋 Early Jurassic

Life on Land

Dinosaurs diversified quickly: large sauropods (Vulcanodon, Shunosaurus) and meat-eating theropods (Dilophosaurus) appeared.Early stegosaurs also emerged.Mammals were still tiny, nocturnal insectivores.

🌋 Early Jurassic

Oceans

Marine reptiles thrived — plesiosaurs, ichthyosaurs, and early crocodiles ruled the seas.Ammonites and belemnites (squid relatives) were common.

🌋 Early Jurassic

Air

Pterosaurs dominated the skies, ranging from small insect-eaters to fish-hunters.

174–163
mya

🌿 Middle Jurassic

Continents & Climate:

The split of Pangaea accelerated — Gondwana (southern continents) and Laurasia (northern continents) drifted apart. Warm, tropical climates spread across much of Earth.

🌿 Middle Jurassic

Land Life

Sauropods grew massive — Brachiosaurus, Cetiosaurus, and Patagosaurus roamed in herds.Huge carnivores like Megalosaurus and Allosaurus emerged as apex predators.Stegosaurs and early ankylosaurs (armored dinosaurs) flourished.

🌿 Middle Jurassic

Plants

Conifer forests dominated, with cycads, ginkgoes, and ferns spreading under the canopy.

🌿 Middle Jurassic

Marine Life

Giant marine reptiles like pliosaurs (Liopleurodon) appeared.Coral reefs expanded, supporting diverse fish and invertebrate life.

163–145
mya

🦖 Late Jurassic

Dinosaurs at Their Peak

The Late Jurassic saw the rise of some of the most iconic dinosaurs. The Sauropods like Diplodocus, Apatosaurus (formerly Brontosaurus), Brachiosaurus. Theropods like Allosaurus (top predator), early relatives of raptors and birds and Ornithischians like Stegosaurus with its plates and spikes.

🦖 Late Jurassic

The First Birds (~150 mya)

Archaeopteryx evolved in what is now Germany, showing feathers, wings, and dinosaur traits — the earliest known bird.

🦖 Late Jurassic

Mammals

Still small, but more diverse: some ate insects, others plants or meat. They stayed in the shadows of dinosaurs.

🦖 Late Jurassic

Marine World

Oceans were dominated by ichthyosaurs, plesiosaurs, and giant pliosaurs.Ammonites were abundant and diverse. The first modern sharks began to appear.

145
mya

🌍 End of the Jurassic

~145 million years ago

The Jurassic ended without a major extinction event (unlike the Triassic before or the Cretaceous after). Instead, it smoothly transitioned into the Cretaceous Period, where flowering plants appeared, and dinosaurs continued to dominate.
