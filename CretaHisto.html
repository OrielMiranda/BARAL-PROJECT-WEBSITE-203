🌍 The Cretaceous Period

145 – 66 million years ago

The Cretaceous Period lasted about 79 million years, from 145 mya to 66 mya. It was the longest period of the Mesozoic Era and ended with the famous mass extinction that wiped out the non-avian dinosaurs.

145–100
mya

🌋 Early Cretaceous

Continents & Climate

Continents continued drifting apart; Gondwana began to split into South America, Africa, Antarctica, and Australia.Warm climate with rising sea levels → shallow seas flooded many lowlands.

🌋 Early Cretaceous

Plants

Flowering plants (angiosperms) first appeared ~130 mya. They spread rapidly and began replacing conifers and cycads in many regions.This plant revolution transformed ecosystems and provided new food sources for herbivores.

🌋 Early Cretaceous

Dinosaurs

Sauropods still roamed, especially in South America. New ornithopods like Iguanodon evolved. Carnivores included Neovenator and early tyrannosaur ancestors (smaller than their later relatives).

🌋 Early Cretaceous

Mammals

Still small but diversifying into insectivores, omnivores, and burrowers. Some early placental and marsupial lineages appeared.

🌋 Early Cretaceous

Oceans

Marine reptiles like plesiosaurs and pliosaurs dominated. Early mosasaurs (giant marine lizards) began to appear near the end of the Early Cretaceous. Ammonites remained abundant.

100–66
mya

🌿 Late Cretaceous

Continents & Climate

Continents closer to their modern positions; wide shallow seas split North America into two landmasses (Laramidia and Appalachia).Climate was very warm, with no permanent polar ice caps.

🌿 Late Cretaceous

Plants

Flowering plants became dominant worldwide, supporting more diverse herbivores and pollinators (early bees and insects).

🌿 Late Cretaceous

Dinosaurs

This period marked the peak of dinosaur diversity. In the south, giant sauropods such as the titanosaurs Argentinosaurus and Alamosaurus ruled the landscapes. At the same time, ornithischian dinosaurs thrived, with duck-billed species like Parasaurolophus and Edmontosaurus alongside horned dinosaurs including Triceratops and Styracosaurus. Among the predators, the theropods reigned supreme, with Tyrannosaurus rex dominating North America and Giganotosaurus standing as one of the largest hunters in South America

🌿 Late Cretaceous

Birds

True modern-style birds evolved from small feathered theropods, diversifying into many forms.

🌿 Late Cretaceous

Mammals

Small but increasingly varied, including early primates, multituberculates, and marsupials. Still overshadowed by dinosaurs.

🌿 Late Cretaceous

Marine Life

Mosasaurs became the dominant marine predators, alongside plesiosaurs and sharks. Ammonites thrived until the very end.

🌿 Late Cretaceous

Air

Pterosaurs included giants like Quetzalcoatlus, one of the largest flying creatures ever. Birds diversified further, some competing with small pterosaurs.

66
mya

🌍 End-Cretaceous Mass Extinction

66 million years ago

About 75% of all species went extinct, including all non-avian dinosaurs, pterosaurs, ammonites, and most marine reptiles.

🌍 End-Cretaceous Mass Extinction

Causes

Asteroid impact created global wildfires, blocked sunlight, and caused a “nuclear winter.” Volcanic activity in India’s Deccan Traps released massive CO₂ and sulfur gases. Climate change + sea level drops added stress.

🌍 End-Cretaceous Mass Extinction

Survivors

Birds (avian dinosaurs), small mammals, crocodiles, turtles, and some marine life endured, setting the stage for the Cenozoic Era — the Age of Mammals.
